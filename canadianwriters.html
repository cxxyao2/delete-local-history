The author of "The Handmaid's Tale" is Margaret Atwood. Margaret Atwood is a Canadian author, poet, and literary critic who has been writing for over five decades. She is considered one of the most important feminist writers of the 20th century and has won numerous awards for her work, including the Booker Prize. "The Handmaid's Tale" is one of her most famous novels and has been widely read and studied since its publication in 1985. The novel is set in a dystopian future where women have been stripped of their rights and freedoms, and is considered a powerful commentary on the status of women in society.
